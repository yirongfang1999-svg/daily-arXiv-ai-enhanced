Abstract: We propose a shock-based framework to interpret the radio and X-ray emission in active galactic nuclei (AGNs), whose origin remains an open problem. In this framework, the radio emission is produced by synchrotron radiation in the accretion flow or a jet/weak outflow, while the dominant X-ray component depends on the accretion state, the location of the non-thermal emission region, and the available seed photon field.
