Among finite-size effects, tidal interactions play a central role, as they are associated to various kinds of deformations of a body under the influence of external tidal fields. In this work, we analyze the dynamical tidal response of Kerr black holes to generic-spin perturbations, focusing primarily on the scalar and gravitational cases, and working to linear order in frequency. We establish an EFT description of the perturbed black hole that accounts for the couplings between the spin, gravitoelectric and -magnetic tidal fields. We match this to wave-like solutions to the full black hole perturbation equations in order to determine the tidal response coefficients.
